Grammy Voting Process

Music companies and individuals must register with the Academy for music to be considered. Once entered, industry experts determine the recording is entered in the right category. Voting members then nominate eligible entries for General Field categories. The final voting ballots are distributed to voting members, and the recording with the most votes in each category are selected to win.

A History of the Grammy Awards

1950s

Recording executives from the Hollywood Walk of Fame committee realized the amount of unrecognized musical talent in the industry since the walk of fame project is awarded only to limited number of persons every year.

1957

The Recording Academy, or the National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences, is officially established.

1959

The first award ceremony was held in two locations on may 4th, the Beverly Hilton Hotel in Beverly Hill, CA and the Park Sheraton Hotel in New York City. The second award ceremony also in 1959 was the first to be televised.

1971

The 13th Grammy Awards were aired live and started the tradition for years to come.

2012

The Recording Academy announced a large restructuring of their award categories, eliminating various categories such as the gender distinction in genre fields and instrumental artist awards. The number of catgories was reduced from 109 to 78.

2000

The Staples Center in Los Angeles, CA becomes the designated venue for the Grammy Award Ceremony.
